 FAMOUS STRATEGIES:
There are four main startegies to playing the Big Brother Game.
  • Comp Beast: Winning is the most important thing when you have this strategy. Every competiton, you are trying to win no matter how big of a target you become. Because if you win, no matter what you are safe. So, you have your own fate in your own hands. If you trust your physical ability, then you will prevail. (Example: Season 20 Winner Kaycee Clark: 5 Veto Wins and 2 HOH wins)
  • Playing Both Sides/Jury Management: This strategy requires you to stay friendly with everyone. If there are two main alliances, you have to play nice with both groups so that you are not the target for either of them. If you are likeable and on good terms with everyone in the house, the other houseguests will be likely to keep you because you are a friend. Be friendly so others feel bad about putting you up. This strategy is also good at keeping the jury members happy with you if you make it to the end, as you have less people bitter over you. (Example: Season 16 Winner Derrick Lavasser: Never nominated once for eviction).
  • Alliances: Having the numbers in Big Brother is everything. If you have a powerful group backing you up and guarenteeing you safety every week, then your work load in the house is halved. You can glide through the game while your group does the work for you, then fight them at the end! It is almost impossible to go through and win the game without some form of an alliance or Final Two (Example: Season 17 winner Steve Moses).
  • Floating: Flying under the radar is a risky, yet rewarding strategy that will keep you in the game for the longest time possible. By not winning many competitions and not appearing to be a good strategist, houseguests will think you are weak and see no reason to evict you, as they think they can beat you easily. So not winning too many competitons or being involved in decisons straight away may be the best way to go, as you get absolutely no blood on your hands (Example: Season 15 Winner Andy Herrren).



KEY TERMINOLOGY
Below is a list of fundamental slang and official vocabulary of the Big Brother game.
  • Alliance
    • A group of houseguests that work togther to take out other houseguests. These can range from 2 to 8 people, and normally have a name (i.e. Level 6, The Moving Company, Bomb Squad, etc)
  • Bitter
    • A jury member that feels angry about something that happened in the house and feels hatred towards someone who outplayed them, ultimately leading to them voting against them.
  • Backdoor
    • When the HOH purposely leaves a houseguest not nominated so they do not play for safety in the Power of Veto competition. The HOH instead nominates them at the Veto meeting.
  • Blindside
    • When houseguests are fooled into thinking something will happen, but the complete opposite happens while they had no idea (normally an eviction).
  • Block
    • The term for where the nomiees who are put for eviction sit on. Whoever is on the block is not safe.
  • Blood
    • When an HOH or any houseguest has to do a dirty tactic in order to get further in the game. This can be lying to someone, putting up an alliance member, or taking out a strong player.
  • Casual
    • A viewer of the show who only watches the epidsodes and not the live feeds.
  • Diary Room
    • The only private room, other than the toilet, in the house. Houseguests are called into the Diary Room occasionally throughout the season in order to answer questions from producers, find out secret powers, or even cry a little. DR (Diary Room) sesssions are only shown on TV and not on the feeds.
  • Double Eviction
    • A special Big Brother episode that happens once a season where two housguests go home in one night after an HOH and Veto competioton is played live.
  • Have-Not
    • A Big Brother punishment where four houseguests have to, for a week, sleep in uncomfortable beds, take cold showers, and eat slop (Most houseguests have to be one at least once).
  • HOH
    • Head of Household. The person who is safe from eviction and nominates two people for the block. The outgoing HOH cannot compete in the next HOH competition.
  • Houseguest
    • A person that plays the game.
  • Jury
    • The evicted houseguests who vote on who should win Big Brother. If the houseguests make Jury, they are sent to another house that is out of the game where they can relax until the finale.
  • Floater
    • A houseguest who does not win many competitions, does not have an alliance, and generally is only existing in the Big Brother house without any strategy.
  • Final Two
    • An alliance made up of 2 people who swear to take each other to the end.
  • Nominee
    • A houseguest put on the block and up for eviction.
  • Pawn
    • A houseguest put up with the target who is not meant to be voted out, but to ensure that the target goes home.
  • Power of Veto
    • A classic Big Brother power with every eviction where the winner can take someone off the block and stay safe. The two nominees and HOH, along with 3 randomly chosen housguests, play for it.
  • Showmance
    • A romantic relationship between two houseguests.
  • Slop
    • The bad and cold food Have-Nots eat
  • Superfan
    • A person who is a geek about Big Brother and has watched every single show since the beginning.
  • Target
    • The nominee the HOH intends on sending home, normally because they are a strong player.
  • Throwing
    • Losing a competition on purpose.
  • Vet
    • An old player from a past season.
  • Walker/Self-Evict
    • A houseguest who chosens to leave the Big Brother house on their own accord, normally done when the emotional mindset is extremly unstable (This has only happened once)
  • Zingbot
    • A robot who visits the Big Brother House every year to make fun of the houseguests remaining.

  • Having Emotional Stability
  • Securing an Alliance
  • Being friendly with everyone and a good social game
  • Have in mind who is your target
  • Staying true to yourself
  • Be competitive and strong
  • Think about what you're playing for
