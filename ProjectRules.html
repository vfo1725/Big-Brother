What is Big Brother?
Goal of the Game:

Big brother is a reality televison game show and social experiment. A group of sixteen complete strangers are put in a house together with no internet, television, cell phones, or anything apart from their clothes and personal belongings. They cannot communicate with anyone from outside the house and only have each other to interact with. They have to stay in the house and are being watched 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and having everthing recorded to and wacthed. The goal of the game is to be still in the house for nearly 100 days and not getting evicted and win half a million dollars. Other than that, the houseguests just have to live with complete strangers in complete isolation for the time they are in the house. Below are the prize money offers for the players.
  • Winner: $500,000
  • Runner-Up: $50,000
  • America's Favorite Player (Voted): $25,000

Evictions/Elimations:

Throughout each week that the players are in the house, two people will always be up for eviction, these being nominated by the head of household (HOH) at the nomination ceremony. There is then always a veto meeting, in which the power of veto can be used to take someone off the block and a new nominee is nominated for good. Then, every Thursday is eviction night, where the other houseguests remaining cast a vote of who to evict out of the two on the block, or simply not safe in the game. These votes are kept in seacret as the houseguests vote in the Diary Room, and the HOH will only vote in the event of a tie. Once evicted, the houseguest will be faced with an immediate interview with the host, Julie Chen, as they leave the house for the first time.


How the Winner is Decided:

The first five evictiees are out of the game for good, but everyone who is evicted afterwards is a part of the jury, the houseguests who vote on who will win. The evictions happen every week and last all the way up until there are two houseguests left. Once at the end, the jury will vote on a person to win the game, and only those people will vote on a winner.

 Compeitions:
There are three main competitions that are always played in the Big Brother Game, two of them are weekly, while the last one happens only once. Competions can range from physical, endurance or mental competitions.

Head Of Household: The head oh household competition happens every week after the live eviction. This is the most important competition for someone to win, as the winner has the power for the week. The HOH is safe for the week and has to nominate two people for eviction. They also sleep in a special bedroom on the second floor which is private and luxorious compared to the rest of the house. The HOH then, though, cannot compete in the next week's HOH competition.

Power of Veto: The second most important competition that is played after the nomination ceremony, and whoever wins the veto is not only safe for the week, but also has the option to take a nominee off the block. They have the option not the use the golden power of veto, but if they do, the HOH is forced into naming a new nominee on the spot. The two nominees and HOH will always play in the veto in addition to three other randomly selected houseguests.

Battle Back: There has been a battle back in each of the past 5 seasons of Big Brother. This competiton happens once a season, and it gives a group of evicted houseguests a chance of coming back into the game. So even though a houseguest can be evicted, one houseguest can get another chance at winnning the game.

Classic Big Brother Comps:
These competitons are reoccuring competitons that appear once each season.

The Wall:
The ultimate endurance competition. The houseguests have to stand on a thin platform along on wall, this while having all sorts of different objects thrown at them. This can range from slime, foam, tennis balls, and more. This competition ends when there is only one houseguest remaining standing on the wall. These competitons normally go on for hours, so it truly tests the contestants physical ability.

OTEV:
OTEV is a famous veto competition that is played each season. This normally happens after the jury has started and some houseguests have already been evicted. A big talking animal, which is different every season, says a clue about a former houseguest, and then the houseguests have to search for the card of that houseguest in the competition area. The game works like musical chairs, though, the last one who returns to OTEV, even if they have the right card, will be eliminated. The competition always runs down until there are two people left, and the last one wins the veto.
Slippery Slope:
The slippery slope competition is another endurance competition, but this involves a lot of slipping and sliding. The houseguets have to run back and forth across a slippery pathway, normally made of ice, and transpot a spoonful of liquid. The goal is to fill up a container on one side of the path with the liquid on the other side of thep path, so the houseguests need to bring the liquid over by spoon without falling down. The competition can last hours as the houseguests gradually fill their container.


Stay or Fold:
The math competition that relies on intuition and problem solving. The houseguests are given a small sample to look and are told how many objects are in that small sample (i.e. Jellybeans). They are then shown a much larger sample, and the houseguests have to write down how many are in the large sample. The trick is though, the houseguests can either Stay or Fold. Staying means that you are confident in your answer so that you want your answer to count, or if you Fold, your answer will not count for that round but you are safe. Out of the people who stay, the furthest. guesser is elimated, and the closest guesser is awarded a ticket. The houseguests who gets 3 tickets first wins.
