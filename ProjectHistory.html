The Past Decade of Big Brother:
Read below for a quick recap of how the game has evolved in the past 10 seasons of Big Brother:

Dan Gheesling (Season 10 Winner)
 Season 10:
Dan Gheelsing won this season and was the only player in history to play the perfect game. This being that he was never nominated once for eviction and won against Memphis Garrett by a jury vote of 7-0. This season was themed around "classics" and although the game had no major twists, there was constant fights that would happen in each episode as the house was completely divided. This season had 13 houseguests and lasted 71 days.
 Season 11:
This seaon was won by Jordan Lloyd as she beat Natalie Martinez by a vote of 5-2. This season is known for having one of the most unlikable casts in Big Brother History, but also had one of the most iconic moments in Big Brother's first expulsion. Houseguest Chima was expelled from the game as she refused to put on her microphone when instructed to do so, and in fact threw it in the pool in the backyard. This left the game with a bad taste and saw Jordan win the game.

Rachel Reilly (Season 13 Winner)
 Season 12:
Season 12 saw Hayden Moss win against Lane Elenburg by a score of 4-3. This season increased to last 71 days and had 13 houseguests. This season's cast absolutely HATED each other, which left each episode with a fight to watch and brought countless moments of hate. This season also had infamous housguest Rachel Reilly, who said the famous quote, "Floaters, you better grab a lifevest". This changed how floaters would now be targeted forever in the game.
 Season 13:
This season was organized a bit differently from the other seasons of the past as the cast was made up of both new players and old returning players from previous seasons. The newbies got evicted one by one almost immediately and saw the veterans fight it out amongst the 7 of them. Rachel Reilly won against Porsche Riggs by a vote of 4-3, this being the first time the winner was decided by just one vote.

Ian Terry (Season 14 Winner)
 Season 14:
Season 14 was record breaking as it increased the number of houseguests to 16 and increased the duration of the game to 80 days. This season, like Season 13, had a mix of veterans and newbies. One of the four veterans to return was Dan Gheesling, and he pulled off what is argued to be the best move in Big Brother history. This move had Dan act out a funeral after he was certain to go home, but the funeral won the sympathy of everyone in the house and he satyed. Dan made it to the end, but lost to Ian Terry 6-1 and a bitter jury.
 Season 15:
The most controversial season of Big Brother to date due to the houseguests blatant racism, homophobic remarks, and bullying in the show. The cast consited of all new players, so some were inexperienced with the game. This season was also the first to offer a Battle Back competition where four jury members competed to come back in the game. Andy Herren won against GinaMarie Zimmerman by a vote of 7-2. He was also the first openly gay player to win Big Brother.

Derrick Levasseur (Season 16 Winner)
 Season 16:
One of the most enjoyable seasons to watch for casuals (people who only watch the episodes), due to the many altering personalities in the house. Derrick Levasseur won against his alliance member Cody Calafiore by a vote of 7-2. Derrick and Cody formed an alliance called "The Hitmen", and controlled the whole game, being one of only two Final Two alliances to make it to the end. Derrick is also considered to be alongside Dan Gheelsing for the greatest Big Brother player of all time, as he was not nominated once.
 Season 17:
One of the more recent seasons with higher reviews and not too many controversial moments as Steve Moses won against Liz Nolan by a vote of 6-3. This season was adventorous in introducing a season twist called the twin twist, in which two identical housguest twins would secretly switch in and out of the house until the jury started. After this, Liz and Julia Nolan  were both in the game. Furthermore, Steve, the winner, is known to have been the biggest floater to win Big Brother, as he won the fewest competitons of all winners (3).

Paul Abrahamian (Season 18 & 19 Runner-Up)
 Season 18:
Season 18 of Big Brother saw returning veteran from Season 16 Nicole Franzel win against Paul Abrahamian by a score of 5-4. This season was of the longest in Big Brother history as it lasted 99 days. No major twists happened this season to add onto the original game, but it was the first season to have TWO battle back competitions. Astonishingly, houseguest Victor Arroyo won BOTH of the battle backs, meaning he was evicted three times before gone for good. Nicole, the showmance of Victor, won against Paul in the end.
 Season 19:
Season 19 is not regarded well by the Big Brother fanbase and is deemed to be one of the worst seasons that the show has ever had. This is because the game pratically revolved all around Paul Abrahamian, a returning veteran of last season, as he was the sole dictator of the house. He controlled every single eviction and all of the other houseguests did what he wanted. Paul, however, lost incredibly AGAIN in the final by the SAME score of 5-4 to the eventual winner, Josh Martinez.

Kaycee Clark (Season 20 Winner)
 Season 20:

The most recent season of Big Brother saw Kaycee Clark beat alliance memeber Tyler Crispen by a vote of 5-4. This season imporved on the past seasons as the house was completely divided and blindsides were occuring each week. Also, one of the worst and one of the best alliances in Big Brother history was formed. The FOUTTE alliance lasted less than a week and fell apart immediately, while the Level 6 alliance controlled most of the game all the way until the end. This season had a likebale cast, comdeic moments, and interesting fights which made ratings for the show increase for the first time in several seasons.



Past Global Headlines:

Racism:

The biggest headline that Big Brother has made has been due to racism. As the houseguests in the game are being recorded at all times for a long period of time, some houseguests would be caught saying racist remarks towards other houseguests. This epidemic mainly started in Season 15 with houseguest Aaryn Gries, a young college student who lost her job and college education due to several racist things she said on the show. Ever since then, racism has been picked up on in previous and future seasons, leaving the concern if racism is still a vital issue in today's society. See the interview with Aaryn and visit the first global webpage on the subject. Article from Big Brother 15.

Cheating/Adultery:

Another issue that has arised with the show is that once houseguests, that already have a significant other, enter the house, there is an immense amount of flirting in the house. This leads to some showmances that end long-term relationships outside the house. This was especially seen in Big Brother 16, where married Christine Varner flirted intensely with young Cody Calafiore, this leaving viewers and the husband angry as Christine was booed when she was evicted. This issue occurs less in recent seasons, but can damage a houseguests life for certain.

Bullying:

A common tactic in the Big Brother game is to lie and backstab people, but everyone knows this is a part of the game. However, there have been some instances in past seasons where houseguests have gone too far with degrading another houseguest where it goes beyond the game. Bullying takes place out of hatred more than trying the get further in the game, this also emerging in Big Brother 15 with Amanda Zuckerman terrorizing Elissa Reily on deep personal levels.
